"Wood is the only material that was alive. That matters."

I picked up a chisel at seventeen in my uncle's workshop in Ibadan. What started as summer labour became a lifelong conversation with material — the weight of grain under a hand plane, the way white oak splits differently in harmattan air than in rain.

After training under master carpenter Emmanuel Addo in Accra, I returned to Ibadan and opened Abbey Craft in 2006. We've since made pieces for private residences, boutique hotels, galleries and embassies across West Africa and Europe.

Every commission begins with a site visit, a long conversation, and usually a cup of tea. We don't rush. You'll wait six weeks for a piece. You'll keep it forever.
